## Multiple Representations of Points
$$(r,\theta) = (r,\theta \pm 2n\pi)$$
$$(r, \theta) = (-r,\theta \pm (2n+1)\pi)$$

## Conversion
![diagram](https://learn.flvs.net/webdav/educator_precalc_v12/module08/imagmod8/08_06_08.gif)
$$x=r\cos(\theta)$$
$$y=r\sin(\theta)$$
$$\tan(\theta)=\frac{y}{x}$$
$$r^2=x^2+y^2$$
